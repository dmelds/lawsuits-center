What Are Medical Device Lawsuits?

Medical device lawsuits generally involve allegations that a device caused injury because of a defect, failure, warning issue, recall, contamination, design problem, or other safety concern.

These claims may involve implanted devices, surgical mesh, joint replacements, cardiac devices, filters, pumps, monitors, or other products used in medical care.

Information That May Help a Review

  • The device name, manufacturer, model, serial number, or implant card information
  • Date of implantation, surgery, removal, revision, or complication
  • Medical records, operative reports, imaging, or device records
  • Symptoms, diagnosis, complications, or additional surgeries
  • Whether the device was recalled or identified in warnings or safety communications

Medical device claims can be highly fact-specific and often require careful review of medical records, product identification, timing, and state law.
