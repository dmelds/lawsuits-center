Who may use this page

People diagnosed with illnesses sometimes linked to asbestos exposure, including those researching occupational, secondhand, military, shipyard, refinery, construction, factory, or industrial exposure histories.

What people often want to know

Whether the diagnosis, work history, timing, and exposure background may justify further review by someone handling these matters.

What happens here

You can submit basic information for a case review request. That information may be used to evaluate whether your situation should be considered for follow-up.

Situations people often research

  • Mesothelioma after past asbestos exposure
  • Lung cancer after known or suspected asbestos exposure
  • Asbestosis or serious respiratory disease tied to past work conditions
  • Exposure in shipyards, plants, refineries, industrial sites, construction, demolition, or military service
  • Secondhand asbestos exposure through a family member’s work clothes or equipment
  • Questions about old job sites, products, insulation, boilers, pumps, valves, gaskets, or other industrial materials
