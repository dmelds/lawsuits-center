What Are Product Liability Lawsuits?

Product liability lawsuits generally involve allegations that a product caused injury because of a design defect, manufacturing defect, failure to warn, misleading labeling, contamination, or another safety issue.

These cases may involve consumer products, household items, industrial products, medical products, tools, vehicles, equipment, or other items used by individuals, workers, patients, or consumers.

Common Product Liability Categories

Medical Device Lawsuits

Claims involving implanted or external medical devices, complications, recalls, or alleged defects.

Drug Injury Lawsuits

Claims involving prescription medications, reported side effects, warnings, and alleged injuries.

Roundup Lawsuit

Claims involving glyphosate-based herbicides and alleged links to certain cancers.

Ozempic / GLP-1 Lawsuits

Claims involving certain medications and reported gastrointestinal complications or related injuries.

Social Media Addiction Lawsuit

Claims involving allegations that certain social media platforms were designed to encourage compulsive use and contributed to youth mental-health harms.

Information That May Matter

  • The product name, manufacturer, model, lot number, or prescription details
  • When and how the product was used
  • The injury, diagnosis, complication, or loss involved
  • Medical records, receipts, photos, labels, packaging, or warnings
  • Whether there were recalls, lawsuits, investigations, or similar reports

Product liability cases can depend heavily on facts, timing, product identification, medical history, and state law.
