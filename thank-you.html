What May Happen Next

  1. Your submission may be reviewed for category fit. The information you provided may be reviewed to determine whether it appears to match an available lawsuit category, claim pattern, or case review path.
  2. You may be contacted for more information. If your situation appears to match an available review path, a participating attorney, legal advertiser, or intake partner may contact you by phone, email, or text message.
  3. Please watch for unfamiliar numbers. Case review calls may come from a number you do not recognize. If a voicemail is left, calling back promptly may help avoid delays.
  4. No outcome is guaranteed. A submission does not guarantee eligibility, compensation, representation, contact, or that any law firm will accept your matter.

While you wait for follow-up, do not delay if you believe a legal deadline may apply to your situation. You may also contact a licensed attorney directly.

What to Gather While You Wait

Having basic information ready may make any follow-up call more useful. You do not need to have everything, but these details may help.

Dates and Timeline

Approximate dates of exposure, product use, medication use, diagnosis, injury, accident, or other key events.

Medical or Injury Information

The name of any diagnosis, injury, symptoms, treatment, doctor visit, hospitalization, or medical facility involved.

Exposure or Product Details

Product names, job sites, employers, locations, medications, devices, contaminated areas, or other details connected to your situation.

Documents and Records

Photos, receipts, employment records, medical summaries, product packaging, bills, letters, or other records that may help explain what happened.

Contact Readiness

Keep your phone nearby and check voicemail, email, and text messages. A follow-up call may come from an unfamiliar number.

Questions You Want to Ask

Write down what you want to know, including timing, possible next steps, what information is needed, and whether a deadline may apply.
