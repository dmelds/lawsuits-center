Self-Check

Do you recognize your situation?

Many lawsuit reviews begin with a pattern: a person used a product, took a medication, worked around a substance, lived near contamination, or experienced an injury — and later learned that others reported similar issues.

If any of the following describe your situation, a case review request may be a reasonable next step:

  • You used a product, took a medication, or were around a substance you now have concerns about.
  • You have been diagnosed with a condition or experienced an injury since then.
  • You have seen news, ads, lawsuits, or other people describing similar situations.
  • You have records, dates, photos, medical information, or other documentation from around that time.
Continue to Free Case Review

A case review request does not guarantee eligibility, compensation, or representation.

How We Work

What Lawsuit Center is — and isn't.

Lawsuit Center is an attorney-built research and connection layer. We organize active lawsuit categories, document what kinds of situations may fit each one, and connect people who submit information to law firms with active intake in the relevant category.

We are not a law firm and we don't provide legal advice. That independence is the point: we don't sign cases, we don't take fees out of any recovery, and we don't represent anyone. When firms pay for visibility on a category page, we label it. Editorial categorization stays separate from paid placement.

Legal deadlines can be short. If you believe you may have a claim, consider speaking with a licensed attorney as soon as possible.
