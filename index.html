How Case Review Works

  1. Choose a lawsuit category. Start by reviewing the type of claim, exposure, product, condition, or injury involved.
  2. Compare your situation to common patterns. Many lawsuits involve repeated exposure, similar injuries, shared products, or similar factual circumstances.
  3. Request a case review if available. If a case review option is available, you may submit basic information for review.
  4. A participating firm or legal advertiser may contact you. Submitting information does not guarantee that you qualify for a claim or that any law firm will offer representation.

Do You Recognize Your Situation?

Many lawsuits begin with patterns. A person may have been exposed to a product, chemical, workplace condition, medication, device, or environment and later learn that others have reported similar experiences.

  • Exposure to a chemical, product, medication, device, or unsafe condition
  • A diagnosis, injury, or loss that may relate to that exposure or event
  • Other people reporting similar issues, lawsuits, or investigations
  • Records, dates, photos, medical information, or other documents that may help explain what happened

If that sounds familiar, you may want to keep researching or learn how case review works.
