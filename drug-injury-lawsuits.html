What Are Drug Injury Lawsuits?

Drug injury lawsuits generally involve allegations that a prescription medication, over-the-counter drug, or related pharmaceutical product caused harm because of side effects, inadequate warnings, labeling issues, design concerns, or other alleged problems.

These claims often require careful review of medical history, prescribing information, timing of use, diagnosis, warnings, and whether other people have reported similar issues.

Information That May Help a Review

  • The medication name, dose, and prescribing history
  • Approximate dates of use
  • The diagnosis, complication, or injury involved
  • Medical records, pharmacy records, or prescription history
  • Whether symptoms began during or after use of the medication
  • Whether a doctor linked the issue to the medication

Medication-related claims are fact-specific. A case review request does not mean that a medication caused an injury or that a legal claim exists.
