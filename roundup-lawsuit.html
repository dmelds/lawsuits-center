Roundup Lawsuit

Roundup lawsuits involve claims that glyphosate-based herbicides may be linked to certain cancers, including non-Hodgkin lymphoma.

What Are Roundup Lawsuits?

Thousands of lawsuits have been filed alleging that manufacturers failed to adequately warn users about potential health risks associated with long-term exposure.

These cases are often discussed more broadly as part of active lawsuits involving toxic exposure and product-related claims.

Who May Qualify

  • People who regularly used Roundup or similar glyphosate-based herbicides
  • Individuals with repeated occupational, agricultural, or residential exposure
  • Those later diagnosed with certain cancers, including non-Hodgkin lymphoma

Common Exposure Situations

  • Landscaping or groundskeeping work
  • Agricultural or farming use
  • Routine spraying around homes, properties, or job sites
  • Long-term exposure over months or years

Why People Are Exploring Claims

Individuals who regularly used glyphosate herbicides and later developed serious medical conditions may be exploring whether they have legal options.
