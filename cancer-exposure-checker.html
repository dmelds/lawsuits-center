What This Checks

Patterns the tool looks for.

Cancer-related lawsuits often involve allegations that a product, chemical, environmental contaminant, or toxic exposure contributed to a diagnosis. This checker walks through four short questions about the cancer type or types, suspected exposure, diagnosis timeframe, and state, then maps your answers to the active case categories most commonly associated with that pattern. For a deeper overview, read Cancers Linked to Lawsuits on Lawsuit Informer.

  • Cancer type or types, including mesothelioma, kidney, ovarian, lung, lymphoma, leukemia, bladder, liver, and uterine. Select all that apply.
  • Suspected exposure source, including asbestos, talc, Roundup, hair relaxer, AFFF foam, PFAS, Camp Lejeune water, benzene, and more
  • Approximate diagnosis timeframe, since statutes of limitations can be short
  • State, because deadlines and claim rules vary
